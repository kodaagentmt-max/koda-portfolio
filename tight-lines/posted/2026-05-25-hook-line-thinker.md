Can a fish actually drown? 🐟💦

Here's the deal — fish need oxygen to survive, just like us. They pull O₂ from water through their gills. But if the water has zero oxygen (like in overgrown ponds or ice-covered holes), a fish can absolutely suffocate — commonly called "drowning."

Here's the plot twist: Some fish CAN drown in oxygen-rich water — if their gills get damaged or clogged by parasites or pollutants.
